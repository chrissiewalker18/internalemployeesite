Who will take the home the gold?

Across all Advisory Board International departments, teams of five compete in three Olympic games composed of three Olympic events: OAB lead passing, OAB visit scheduling, and OAB unit closing At the end of each game, the top 3 performing teams will earn medals (gold, silver, and bronze). At the end of the Olympics, the team with the highest medal average takes home the gold medal prize!

Official Olympic Rules and Regulations:

  • Your mixed department team of five will compete in three "Olympic games". At the end of each game, the top three performing teams with the highest point scores will earn medals (gold, silver, and bronze).

    Game 1: Feb. 1 – Feb. 29
    Game 2: Mar. 1 – Mar. 31
    Game 3: Apr. 1 – Apr. 31

  • Over the course of each "game", each member of your team can earn points by passing OAB leads, scheduling OAB visits, and closing OAB units. At the end of each game month, your team's total cumulative point score will determine your country's medal earnings.

    OAB lead = 1 point *leads passed within the current game month
    OAB visit = 3 points*visits scheduled within the current game month
    OAB unit = 5 points *units closedwithin the current game month

  • At the start of each game month, all teams begin with a clean slate at 0 points.
  • At the end of Game 3, the team with the highest medal average wins the gold-level prize, the team with the second highest medal average wins the silver-level prize, and the team with the third highest medal average wins the bronze-level prize.
